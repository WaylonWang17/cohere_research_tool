# Marie Antoinette: Who She Was and Why She Matters Today

## Basic Biography

**Name:** Marie Antoinette Josèphe Jeanne (born Maria Antonia Josefa Johanna)
**Born:** 2 November 1755 at the Hofburg Palace, Vienna, Austria
**Died:** 16 October 1793 at the Place de la Révolution (now Place de la Concorde), Paris, France
**Title:** Queen consort of France (1774-1792)
**Family:** 15th child and youngest daughter of Emperor Francis I and Empress Maria Theresa of Austria
**Marriage:** Married Louis-Auguste (later Louis XVI) in 1770 at age 14

## Historical Significance

Marie Antoinette was the last queen of France before the monarchy was abolished during the French Revolution. Her life represents the dramatic intersection of:
- Royal privilege and revolutionary fury
- Austrian-French political alliances
- Gender politics and public scandal
- Historical myth-making versus documented fact

## Major Life Events

**1774:** Becomes Queen of France when Louis XVI ascends throne
**1789:** Royal family placed under house arrest at Tuileries Palace
**1792:** Monarchy abolished; Louis XVI imprisoned
**1793:** Tried for treason and executed by guillotine during the Reign of Terror

## Notable Misconceptions

- **The Diamond Necklace Affair (1785):** Often blamed on her extravagance, but she was actually innocent - a con artist named Jeanne de la Motte perpetrated the fraud while impersonating her
- **"Let them eat cake":** This famous quote was never actually spoken by her
- **Madame Déficit:** Nickname given by revolutionaries who blamed her for France's financial troubles, though she had limited political power

## Current Relevance (2024)

Marie Antoinette remains culturally significant, evidenced by recent events:

### Jewelry and Artifacts
- **November 2024:** A diamond necklace possibly linked to the 1785 scandal sold at Sotheby's Geneva for $4.81 million
- **November 2024:** Marie Antoinette's jeweled Breguet watch No. 160 goes on display at London's Science Museum in the "Versailles: Science and Splendour" exhibition
- **2018:** A pearl pendant belonging to her sold for $36 million, setting a record for historical jewelry

### Cultural Impact
- **Theatre:** Multiple productions including a dark comedy at Oglethorpe Theatre/Actors Express in Atlanta
- **Museum Exhibitions:** Her private chambers at Versailles have reopened after restoration
- **Academic Interest:** Continued scholarly debate about her role in French Revolution and women's history

## Legacy

Marie Antoinette's story continues to fascinate because she represents:
- The fall of aristocratic power
- Gendered political persecution
- How historical narratives are constructed and reconstructed
- The price of being a woman in power during turbulent times
