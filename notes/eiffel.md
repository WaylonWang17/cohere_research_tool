# Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower (  EYE-fəl; French: Tour Eiffel [tuʁ ɛfɛl] ) is a lattice tower on the Champ de Mars in Paris, France. It is named after the engineer Gustave Eiffel, whose company designed and built the tower from 1887 to 1889.

## Basic Information
- **Location**: Champ de Mars, Paris, France
- **Nickname**: "La dame de fer" (French for "Iron Lady")
- **Construction Period**: 1887-1889
- **Purpose**: Centre piece of the 1889 World's Fair, celebrating the centennial anniversary of the French Revolution

## History
Locally nicknamed "La dame de fer" for its use of wrought iron, it was constructed as the centrepiece of the 1889 World's Fair, and to crown the centennial anniversary of the French Revolution. Although initially criticised by some of France's leading artists and intellectuals for its design, it has since become a global cultural icon of France and one of the most recognisable structures in the world.

## Significance & Records
- **Most visited monument**: With an entrance fee, the most visited monument in the world (6.91 million people ascended it in 2015)
- **Visitors**: 5,889,000 visitors in 2022
- **Designations**: Monument historique (1964), UNESCO World Heritage Site (1991)
- **Tallest structure**: Was the tallest human-made structure in the world for 41 years (1889-1930)

## Physical Specifications
- **Height**: 330 metres (1,083 ft) tall
- **Base**: Square, measuring 125 metres (410 ft) on each side
- **Structure**: First structure to surpass both 200-metre and 300-metre marks
- **Current status**: With broadcasting aerial added in 1957, it's 11 metres taller than the Chrysler Building

## Visitor Experience
- **Levels**: Three levels for visitors
- **Restaurants**: On the first and second levels
- **Observation deck**: 276 m (906 ft) above ground - highest public observation deck in the European Union
- **Access**: Stairs or lift to first and second levels
- **Stairs**: 300 steps from ground to first level, 300 steps from first to second level (total 600 steps)
- **Special feature**: Private apartment on the top level built for Gustave Eiffel

## Cultural Impact
Despite initial criticism from French artists and intellectuals, the Eiffel Tower has become a global cultural icon of France and one of the most recognisable structures worldwide.
